What it replaces

The reporting habits most teams already have — and why they break down

Security incidents still get reported the way everything else does: a Slack message, a forwarded email, a sticky note. None of it survives contact with a second incident.

No. 01

Reports scattered everywhere

Email threads, chat messages, and verbal handoffs make it impossible to see every active incident at once.

No. 02

Status nobody can confirm

Without a shared record, "is this fixed yet?" becomes a question someone has to go ask a person, not a system.

No. 03

No paper trail when it matters

Audits, post-mortems, and compliance reviews all need a documented history that ad-hoc reporting never produces.

What you get

One case file, from report to resolution

Structured intake

Title, category, severity, and description — every report arrives in the same shape, ready to triage.

Lifecycle tracking

Open, In-Progress, Resolved. Every status change is timestamped and attributed automatically.

Evidence locker

Attach screenshots, logs, and documents directly to the case so proof never lives in a separate inbox.

Search & filter

Find a case by keyword, or narrow the list by category, severity, or status in a couple of clicks.

Dashboard analytics

A live count of total, open, in-progress, and resolved incidents — the state of the desk at a glance.

Role-based access

Employees file reports; analysts and admins triage, reassign, and close them under their own login.
